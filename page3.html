Patrick Ewing

Accomplishments

  • 11x NBA All-Star
  • 1990 All-NBA First Team
  • 1986 Rookie of the Year
  • 6x All-NBA Second Team
  • 3x All-NBA Defensive Second Team

    • Who Was "The Beast of the East"?

    Patrick Ewing was a 7 foot center from Kingston, Jamaica. He would end up at Georgetown University to play college basketball. At Georgetown he was able to develop quiet the basketball resume by becoming a 3-time All-American and being named Big East Player of the Year not once, but twice. He would continue his basketball career after college, after being picked first overall in the 1985 draft. He would spend the next 15 years with the Knicks, before spending the last two years of his career with the Seattle Supersonics and the Orlando Magic. Ewing was a member of the 1992 Dream Team and would be inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 2008. Over the course of his career Ewing averaged almost a double-double, with 21.0 points per game, 9.8 rebounds per game and 2.4 blocks per game. While an NBA Championship had always alluded Ewing, he was voted as one of the top 50 greatest basketball players of all time during the 50th Anniversy of the NBA celebration in 1996.
